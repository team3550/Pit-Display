Joint Practices at Valley High School

On Oct. 11, team Border Line Obsessed Technicians, and team Elemental Fusion invited all 5 teams in the West Des Moines district to a joint practice for the evening. Team members from Beta helped the other teams with programming, and many ideas were shared in a collaborative environment

Helping Iowa teams

Throughout this season, we have helped several teams from around Iowa including teams: Sanitize, Up Up Down Down Left Right Left Right B A , Nerd Herd, and Panda X-Press. We helped them with various programming issues and helped most of them get a robot up and running for the first time.

Bears, #3141 webcast

On Sept. 14 and Oct. 19th, FTC team 3141, The Bears invited us to participate in a webcast for all FTC teams. We covered topics such as the use of sensors, team media presence, fundraising, thoughts on the game, lessons learned from prototyping, and more.

West Des Moines, an FTC Legacy

Over the past 9 years, we have worked hard to build an FTC legacy in the West Des Moines area. Seven years ago, no FIRST programs existed in the area, and it has since grown to 8 FTC teams, and 1 FRC team. Support for the FIRST programs is finally beginning to emerge in the school district, and Beta is continuing to work towards creating a sustainable FIRST community for future students.

Beginnings of a STEM Center

In June, members of Beta, community leaders, and key individuals from the city met to begin plans for a STEM center that would support the local FIRST teams, provide opportunities for other STEM programs, and create connections with a Makerspace. Since then, we have visited a potential space to house the STEM center, and have continued developments to realize these plans.

The Yellow Alliance

The Yellow Alliance is an in-progress database of all FTC teams and all FTC matches as the 2013-14 Block Party! season progresses. This is a collaborative effort between Beta, and FTC Iowa to provide useful information and statistics as the season progresses, and to provide metrics on how the game is changing over the season. Data is uploaded from the Scoring System at every event, and the database will grow as the season progresses, providing data and information like FRC's "The Blue Alliance".

U.S. FIRST Judge's Video

After the World Championships in April, we were requested by US FIRST to create an example Judge's Interview video for other teams to model after. The judging panel included the Lieutenant Governor, Kim Reynolds, and was posted by U.S. FIRST on Youtube for all teams to view.

FLL Practice

On Aug. 22nd, four members from our team visited a local FLL team in Urbandale, to talk with them about an Engineering Notebook. We shared our notebook from the previous season, and some information on how to write entries. Then, to promote hands-on-learning, we designed mini-challenge to simulate a competition season, and had them keep track of their "mini-season" in their notebook.

Drivetrain Comparisons

In order to compare a four wheel chassis to a six wheel chassis with dropped center, I created a prototyping platform using the older Lego RCXs. In order to compare the drivetrains side-by-side, and video was compiled from several different tests, and the results are very dramatic. This video was published to youtube so that other teams could observe the differences in drivetrains, and understand the advantages of six driven wheels with the center wheel dropped.
